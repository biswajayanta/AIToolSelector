Traditional RPA —
last resort only
Only when no API exists and modernisation is not possible. RPA automates by mimicking screen clicks — powerful for legacy systems, but fragile and expensive to maintain.
Why it's last resort Any UI change — a button renamed, a screen repositioned — breaks the bot silently. Maintenance costs grow every year. A brittle RPA process creates a technical debt that is hard to retire. Even 2–3 weeks of API enablement will outperform 3 years of RPA maintenance. Engage EA/EAI before committing.
